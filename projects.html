Flower Boutique Mobile Site

Project Overview

With hundreds of flower boutiques on the market, I was part of a team wanting to launch something more modern and simple. I was assigned to design a responsive website for a starting flower boutique in Toronto, Ontario. The team prided itself in wanting to create something that was simple yet chic, translating that into both their flower products and digital presence. Due to the pandemic, the project was put on pause, with user research, wireframes, and a prototype for several screens of the mobile site being complete.

Research and User Flows

The research began with examining other flower boutiques’ sites and flows. For both the sake of inspiration, and seeing what worked well and what didn’t from the perspective of a user who was seeking to purchase a bouquet. Because of the competitive market, during the research stage, it was vital to identify how Rewolf would stand out in the midst of their competitors and how we were to make our user experience different, simple, and aesthetically pleasing.

Wireframes

Taking into consideration the user research, and what the team was seeking to have the boutique’s website look like and feel like to its’ users, I built several versions of pages’ interfaces, offering different options of what the site could look like. Providing my team with a variety of options for what the layout of the application could look like allowed us to decide on the best design for Rewolf.

Prototype

I then began developing a high fidelity prototype for the mobile version of the site. The images used are not ones that will be used by the boutique, but serve as an example of what the visuals of the site would look like if it were complete. Due to the project being put on pause, the prototype has not been finalized, but will likely remain the starting point for when the project is relaunched.
